Unicorn Assassin

The coding bootcamp prework required me to create a game made on the MIT Scratch website based on a set of game rules. The example game shown had a player controled cat that had to catch balls droping from the top of the game screen. The player could move the cat side to side to collide the cat with the ball. If the ball was not caught then the game was over.


My verison of the game was called Unicorn Assasin. The player controlled a unicorn that was able to move up or down the screen and shoot rainbow beams. Instead of colliding with balls as the object of the game, mine was to hit flying gryphons with beams. The grpyhons would appear at random positions of the screen oppsite of the unicorn and fly across. The player would score a point hitting the gryphon or lose a life from either being hit or letting the gryphon reach the end of the screen. The speed at witch the gryphon flies would speed up based on the score and at an certain score a boss gryphon would appear. The boss gryphon had the following functions: set amount of hp, track player, follow player on y-axis, fire lighting bolts when at the same y-axis, flying animations. The game is infinite and only ends if the player losses all their life or exits the game.
